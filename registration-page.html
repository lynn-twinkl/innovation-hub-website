Risks and Considerations for Using Gemini in Google Meets

Purpose

This document outlines the risks and considerations for employees when using Gemini for transcription in Google Meets, ensuring compliance with privacy and data protection standards.

Overview of Gemini

Gemini is a transcription tool integrated with Google Meets that automatically transcribes meetings. While this feature enhances accessibility and documentation, it also raises important privacy and compliance considerations.

Key Considerations

1. Identifying Active Transcription

  • Notification: Employees should be aware when transcription is active during a meeting. Users will receive a notification indicating that transcription is in progress.
  • Transparency: Always inform meeting participants that transcription is enabled, ensuring that all parties are aware their conversations are being recorded.

2. Right to Request Deletion

  • Employee Rights: Employees have the right to request the deletion of any transcription created from a meeting.
  • Procedure: To request deletion, employees should contact their manager or the designated compliance officer. A formal request should include:
    • Date and time of the meeting
    • Participants involved
    • Reason for the deletion request

3. Obligation to Delete Transcriptions

  • Mandatory Compliance: If an employee requests the deletion of a transcription, the responsible party (e.g., meeting host) must ensure that the transcription is deleted promptly.
  • Documentation: Keep a record of all deletion requests and actions taken in response for compliance purposes.

4. Data Privacy Risks

  • Sensitive Information: Be cautious about discussing sensitive or confidential information during meetings, as transcriptions may inadvertently capture such data.
  • Data Breaches: Protect against unauthorized access to transcriptions to prevent potential data breaches. Ensure that only authorized personnel have access to stored transcriptions.

5. Training and Awareness

  • Employee Training: Regular training sessions should be held to ensure employees understand how to use Gemini, the implications of transcription, and their rights and responsibilities.
  • Policy Review: Regularly review compliance policies and update employees on any changes related to the use of transcription tools.

6. Handling Breaches or Incidents

  • Incident Response Plan: Establish a clear incident response plan for data breaches related to transcription data. This plan should outline how to report a breach, the steps to contain it, and how to communicate with affected individuals.
  • Training on Breach Reporting: Provide training to employees on how to recognize and report potential data breaches involving transcriptions.

Conclusion

Using Gemini for Google Meets can enhance meeting productivity and accessibility, but it is essential to remain vigilant about privacy and compliance. By following the outlined procedures and fostering a culture of transparency and respect for privacy, we can leverage this technology responsibly.
